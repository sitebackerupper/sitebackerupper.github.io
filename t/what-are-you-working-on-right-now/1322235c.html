My Sep. idea: Working title: Interplanetary Fauna Protectorate
The Elevator Pitch
You pick and environment in a Durance style way but focused on dangers to both the Fauna Protectorate Patrol (FPP) and the endangered fauna on the planet in a PbtA game. Then, you are given the equivalent of rumors to determine where potential dangers are to this fauna. The game will be mission based but with an overarching story.

Why you’re excited about it
Even though this would obviously be a game about illegal poaching and environmental issues and include the spin out of PbtA games, this game would focus on how the FPP could succeed. This could be through a similar mechanism to the revolution track in Comrades and building up a base of operations. Resources in the base of operations would eventually allow the Patrol to plan ahead and cover more ground and potentially keep ahead of the struggle.

What your hopes are in relation to it
I would love to get it to be both a fun and educational game. Ideally, I would hope to eventually sell it and donate the earnings to the World Wildlife Foundation or a similar charity.

Pitchness:
Towerlands is a fantasy setting based on early-14th-century Hungary but in a parallel world with elves and stuff.

So Excite:
Geography: Pannonian Basin! Carpathian Mountains! Vast limestone caves! Flood plains and swamps! The Danube!

Stuff plucked out of Hungarian folklore and warped for fantasy: Stormdragons ravaging cities! Táltos shamans and druids. Griffins! Fene (illness demons). The World Tree!

Stuff plucked out of Hungarian history and warped for Towerlands: Baron-Oligarchs! The Voivode of Transylvania! Three or four kings all vying for legitimacy! Important crowns! Important cathedrals!

Very Hope:
My first hope is to turn this into a gripping campaign for some local friends, then to expand to run at least one parallel campaign for friends online. My first main goal is to finish a Player’s Guide so that players can make characters and start playing.

Eventually, I’d like to publish products for it.

Haladir September 23, 2019, 9:58pm #125

I am working on “Ultraviolet World,” a hack of Jesse Strandberg’s “Homebrew World” for use in The Ultraviolet Grasslands.

So far, I’m hewing pretty close to Homebrew World, although I am adding back in a few things from vanilla Dungeon World, such as weapon tags and a slightly less-abstract money and treasure system. I am also pulling in a bunch of mechanics from UVG, including a good chunk of the caravan travel system and the buying/selling trade goods system.

I’ve converted all of the playbooks except the Paladin and Weilder (which don’t really fit the setting) and the Druid (needs more customization for the setting). At the request of my players, I made conversions of The Earthling playbook from Adventures on Dungeon Planet and The Con Artist (from a collection of thief character playbooks from an author whose name escapes me at the moment.)

I’ve also written my own version of the HBW Basic and Optional Moves. Most are identical, but I had to tweak a bunch due to the UVG’s overland travel time-scale of one week. The other challenge is getting the right level of abstraction for equipment. Both HBW and UVG do so in very differnt ways, and I’m not sure if I’ve quite squared that circle.

I am in the process of making a new blog where I can post this stuff, and I’ll put up a link when it’s ready for others to see.
